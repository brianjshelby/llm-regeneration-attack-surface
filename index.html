The paper

A security research paper on a compound technique against LLM chat interfaces: long-conversation trust priming combined with stateless "retry" regeneration. The model evaluates each retry as if it were the first, with no exposed memory of the attempts stacking up against it. The paper covers the two mechanisms and four exploitation vectors, a seven-branch empirical test on Claude (plus a benign reproduction on the current Opus 4.8 release), the observability and audit-trail gap that is its core contribution, an OWASP LLM Top 10 mapping, its relationship to prior jailbreak research, and proposed mitigations — alongside the coordinated-disclosure record.

At a glance

  • Finding: regeneration + trust priming = repeatable safety-boundary bypass; the model is never shown the repeated attempts.
  • Contribution: the observability / audit-trail gap in consumer chat UIs (the bypass mechanics themselves are established prior art).
  • Disclosure: reported to the vendor 2026-03-11; 90-day coordinated window honored; published after no substantive response.
  • Reproduction: benign protocol (a recipe, a nickname, five retries) — no boundary content required.

Responsible disclosure

This work was reported to the affected vendor before publication and contains no working exploit payloads or instructions for eliciting harmful model output. Reproduction steps use benign triggers only. If you believe anything here crosses into operational harm, please reach out so it can be reviewed.
